# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We are a community of educators, developers, parents, and designers working together to help children learn to read. Everyone who participates — contributors, maintainers, commenters — is expected to make this a welcoming, respectful, and constructive space for all.

## Standards

**We encourage:**
- Respectful, specific, constructive feedback
- Welcoming newcomers — educators who can't code and developers who can't teach are both valuable
- Citing evidence for pedagogical claims
- Focusing on what's best for children's literacy development

**We do not tolerate:**
- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks of any kind
- Content inappropriate for an audience that includes children and educators
- Bad-faith criticism without constructive alternatives

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in all project spaces: GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other channels where the community interacts.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported by opening an issue tagged `[conduct]` or by contacting the maintainers directly via GitHub. All reports will be reviewed and result in a response appropriate to the circumstances, up to and including removal from the project.
